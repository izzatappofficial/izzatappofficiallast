Example

Domestic violence takes place in 80% of Pakistani households.

Women face significant challenges, such as:
Lack of awareness

Most women do not recognize domestic violence for what it is or view it as a “rural” and far-removed problem. 53% of teenage girls in Pakistan believe domestic violence is justified.

Hostile legal systems

Disputes are seen as a “family matter” and cases aren’t filed unless a killing/attempted killing is involved. Only 2.5% of accused are convicted and women face significant humiliation in the process

Weak social support

Women fear repercussions from their partners/in-laws, don’t get the support they need for fear of bringing dishonour to the family, and are often concerned about their social/economic welfare.

How Faiza Works


Start chatting with Faiza

Say “Hello” to start talking with Faiza. If you indicate you are in an emergency, she will redirect you to emergency resources in your area.

Get guided information

Faiza will provide you with information on the types of domestic abuse and educational guides for situations of various risk levels.

Redirect to resources

We’ll connect you with help in your area, including legal support, hotlines, and shelters for a variety of different situations.
